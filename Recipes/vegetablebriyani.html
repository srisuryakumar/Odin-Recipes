Vegetable Briyani

Vegetable Biryani is a flavorful and aromatic rice dish that originated in the Indian subcontinent. It is a popular vegetarian option and is loved for its combination of fragrant basmati rice, mixed vegetables, and a blend of aromatic spices. The dish is known for its rich and layered flavors, making it a favorite choice for special occasions and gatherings.

Ingredients:

  • 2 cups basmati rice
  • 1large onion, thinly sliced
  • 1 cup mixed vegetables (carrots, peas, beans, etc.), chopped
  • 1/2 cup plain yogurt
  • 1/4 cup chopped mint leaves
  • 1/4 cup chopped coriander leaves
  • 2 tablespoons ghee (clarified butter) or vegetable oil
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • 4 green cardamom pods
  • 4 cloves
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  • 1 teaspoon ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon red chili powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon garam masala
  • Salt to taste
  • Saffron strands (optional)
  • Warm milk (optional)

Instructions:

  1. Rinse the basmati rice in cold water until the water runs clear. Soak the rice in water for 30 minutes, then drain.
  2. In a large pot, heat the ghee or oil over medium heat. Add the cinnamon stick, cardamom pods, cloves, bay leaf, and cumin seeds. Sauté for a minute until fragrant.
  3. Add the sliced onion and cook until it turns golden brown.
  4. Add the ginger-garlic paste and sauté for another minute.
  5. Add the mixed vegetables and cook for a few minutes until they start to soften
  6. In a small bowl, whisk together the yogurt, mint leaves, coriander leaves, turmeric powder, red chili powder, garam masala, and salt.
  7. Add the yogurt mixture to the pot and cook for a couple of minutes, stirring well to coat the vegetables.
  8. Add the drained rice to the pot and gently stir to combine with the vegetables and spices.
  9. Pour enough water into the pot to cover the rice by about an inch. Bring the mixture to a boil.
  10. Reduce the heat to low, cover the pot with a tight-fitting lid, and simmer for 15-20 minutes or until the rice is cooked and fluffy.
  11. If using saffron, soak a few strands in warm milk and drizzle it over the cooked rice for added flavor and color.
  12. Remove the pot from the heat and let it sit, covered, for 5 minutes.
  13. Fluff the rice with a fork, remove the whole spices if desired, and serve the vegetable biryani hot with raita (yogurt sauce) or a side salad.
